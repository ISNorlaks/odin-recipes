Odin Recipes: Pudim

Description:

Brazilian pudding is a creamy dessert, with a slightly yellowish hue and a caramel sauce that adds flavor. This delicacy is believed to have been inspired by priscos pudding, a Portuguese dessert.

However, over time, the pudding went through some variations. Before the popularization of condensed milk, whole milk was one of the main ingredients used to prepare it. It was only in the 19th century that the pudding gained its characteristic ingredients. This combination results in a Brazilian dessert appreciated around the world for its creaminess and flavor, especially when combined with caramel sauce.

Ingredients

  • 3 whole eggs
  • 1 can of condensed milk
  • 1 can of milk (same ammount as condensed milk)

For the sauce:

  • 1 tea cup of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of water

How to prepare

Cooking time: 1h

  1. First, blend well the 3 eggs.
  2. Add the condensed milk and the regular milk, and blend again.
  3. Melt the sugar in a pan until it is brown, then add water and let it thicken.
  4. Put the sauce in a round pan and then add the Pudim's body on top.
  5. Bake in medium heat for 45 minutes, with the round pan inside a bigger pan filled with water.
  6. To see if it is well baked, spike it with a fork.
  7. Let it cool down and put it on a plate of your preference.

That's it!, easy, isn't it?
